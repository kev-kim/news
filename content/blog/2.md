# ![selfie](/images/egg.jpg)

Eggs are possibly the best college food: heat a pan, pour some oil, crack an egg. Or even better, boil some water, add some eggs.

A lot of people are satisfied with just this, but as an avid consumer of eggs, I *needed* to find the perfect recipe. After looking into every possible recipe (and I mean that genuinely), I feel that I found the perfect way to cook eggs for myself. This recipe involves very little time, but involves a few uncommon ingredients and a bit of cooking technique.

A few friends asked me for the recipe of this dish after trying it. So I decided to put it on my website. Why not?

The recipe itself is a simpler version of [Cantonese-style Scrambled Eggs](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ONYflj0I2QI). The main difference is that my recipe uses less ingredients (but still tastes really good!), which makes making these eggs even easier.

**These eggs are a perfect fit to my personal style. They are as soft as a souffle from the shaoxing wine. They have the rich flavor of sesame oil which simultaneously removes the sulfuric aftertaste of the egg. If you don't like your eggs that way, then this recipe isn't for you.**

## Kevin's eggs

Ingredients:
- 4 eggs
- 2 tb sp of sesame oil
- 2 tb sp of shaoxing wine (this is irreplacable!)
- salt
- white pepper (or black pepper)
- green onion, thinly sliced

Cooking:
1. Crack open and pour the eggs in a bowl with the sesame oil, shaoxing wine, salt, and white pepper.
2. Whisk the eggs vigorously. This step is important as it ensures that the egg will be soft when cooked.
3. Preheat a pan over the stove. When the pan is very hot, pour the eggs into the pan.
4. Wait around 7 seconds, and use a spatula to scrape the egg from the edges towards the center. This step is also very important in ensuring the texture of the egg.
5. If done correctly, the rest of the egg mixture will flow to the edges of the pan. Repeat step 4 until egg is almost cooked.
6. Add in the sliced green onions on the egg.
7. Fold the egg in half and wait for 30 seconds.
8. Use the spatula to guide the egg off the pan and serve on a plate with roasted tomatoes.

(Steps 4-5 can be learned from [this video](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ONYflj0I2QI).)

When done correctly, you should now have an immensely flavorful, soft, and filling egg dish.
